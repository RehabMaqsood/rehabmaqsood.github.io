Case Study 32022 — 2025 LUMSx Catalogue

Building a new institution its catalogue — from the ground up.

Pedagogical Research · Format Strategy · Pre-Production · Quality Assurance

Problem Twenty-plus courses across very different subjects — management, technical, soft skills — needed to ship at scale, with no precedent to lean on. The work meant researching what reads on screen, absorbing cutting-edge digital pedagogy, and adapting a wide range of content into a catalogue the institution could keep building on for years.

i. Process · Format Allocation

Format as a pedagogical decision.

High animation, low animation, slide-based — each chosen for what the subject can carry.

Format infrastructure that became permanent: three tiers, every course assigned to one of them on the basis of subject density and learner journey.

Format A
High-end engaging animation

Subject-led illustration, motion graphics, custom characters. For courses where the image carries weight on its own.

Format B
Low-tech, text-and-illustration

Hybrid format with limited animation. Carries dense subject matter without the cost of full motion design.

Format C
Slide-based tutorial & demo

Instructional efficiency at scale. Honest format for technical, demonstration-led, or rapidly-iterating subjects.

ii. Process · Storyboarding

Conceptual direction, then production.

Defining what each frame does.

The storyboard is a blueprint for illustration and animation — and just as much for sequencing, editing, and the shooting plan. It works by absorbing the learning objectives and instructional design strategy, then translating them into video format.
